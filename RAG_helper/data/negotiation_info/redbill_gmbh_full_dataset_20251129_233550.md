## COMPANY OVERVIEW

Red Bill GmbH is a Munich-headquartered technology enterprise at the forefront of Germany’s digital transformation, specializing in enterprise Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), artificial intelligence (AI) automation, and Industry 4.0 solutions. Established to address the evolving needs of European and global mid-market and enterprise clients, Red Bill GmbH has rapidly positioned itself as a pioneering force in the convergence of advanced software, smart manufacturing, and regulatory-driven digitalization. The company’s headquarters, located at Theresienstraße 12, 80333 München, strategically situates Red Bill in the heart of Munich’s vibrant innovation and technology ecosystem, alongside leading universities, research institutions, and DAX-listed industrial titans.

Red Bill GmbH employs 1,847 staff as of 2025, a testament to its robust growth trajectory and commitment to attracting top talent across engineering, data science, product management, and customer success functions. The company’s organizational culture embodies the best of the German Mittelstand ethos—grounded in reliability, long-term relationships, and technical excellence—while simultaneously embracing a global mindset essential for scaling in the international SaaS and AI marketplace. Employees hail from over 38 countries, reflecting Red Bill’s dedication to diversity, cross-cultural collaboration, and fostering a workplace where innovation thrives.

Financially, Red Bill GmbH reported revenues of €478 million for fiscal year 2024, marking its strongest year yet. This figure reflects a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 37% since 2021, driven by expanding enterprise subscriptions, recurring maintenance contracts, and a growing portfolio of AI-powered modules. The company’s revenue mix is well-balanced across its core segments: 53% from enterprise SaaS platforms, 29% from AI automation services, and 18% from Industry 4.0 hardware-software integration solutions. Recurring revenues accounted for 84% of total turnover in 2024, underscoring the resilience and scalability of Red Bill’s business model. EBITDA margins improved to 26.4%, reflecting ongoing investments in R&D and operational efficiency initiatives.

Red Bill GmbH’s corporate vision is anchored in its aspiration to become a DAX 40 constituent by 2030. With a clear path to public listing, the company’s leadership has implemented rigorous governance, transparent reporting, and a scalable operational framework designed to meet the demands of public markets and global institutional investors. This ambition is further supported by a high-profile Supervisory Board comprising former executives from DAX companies, seasoned technology entrepreneurs, and compliance experts. The company’s financial strategy prioritizes sustainable growth, robust cash flows, and prudent capital allocation, with significant reinvestment into product innovation and international expansion.

At the core of Red Bill GmbH’s value proposition is its portfolio of enterprise SaaS platforms, each engineered for mission-critical business processes in manufacturing, logistics, energy, and the broader industrial sector. The company’s flagship products, such as RedOps AI (an intelligent operations management suite), BillCore ERP (a modular enterprise resource planning system), and the RedConnect IoT Gateway (for secure device integration), have been widely adopted by German Mittelstand champions and multinational corporations alike. These platforms are designed for seamless integration with legacy IT environments, high configurability, and adherence to stringent European data privacy and cybersecurity standards.

A defining pillar of Red Bill GmbH’s market differentiation is its leadership in AI automation. Leveraging proprietary machine learning algorithms and real-time analytics engines, Red Bill delivers solutions that enable predictive maintenance, intelligent supply chain orchestration, and adaptive production scheduling. The company’s AI modules are developed in collaboration with leading German research institutes, ensuring that innovations are not only cutting-edge but also tailored to the realities of regulated industrial environments. Red Bill’s AI Center of Excellence, based at its Munich HQ, serves as both a research hub and a customer co-innovation space, driving continuous improvement and rapid prototyping.

Industry 4.0 represents a major strategic focus for Red Bill GmbH. Recognizing the transformative impact of digitized manufacturing, the company provides end-to-end solutions that connect shop floor assets, automate quality assurance, and enable data-driven operational excellence. Red Bill’s Industry 4.0 deployments span automotive, aerospace, chemical, and energy verticals, with reference projects including smart factory rollouts for several MDAX and DAX 40 manufacturers. The company’s integration services are supported by a network of certified partners across Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and the Benelux region, ensuring high-touch implementation and long-term customer success.

Regulatory compliance is deeply embedded in Red Bill GmbH’s DNA. As a European-headquartered SaaS provider, Red Bill is fully compliant with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), offering clients robust data residency, encryption, and auditability features. In anticipation of the EU’s Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), the company has invested heavily in operational risk management, incident response automation, and third-party vendor oversight. Red Bill’s solutions are regularly audited by external cybersecurity firms and certified to ISO/IEC 27001 and TISAX standards, providing assurance to clients in highly regulated industries such as finance, automotive, and pharmaceuticals. The company’s Data Protection Office, led by a former regulator, works closely with product teams to ensure that privacy-by-design principles are embedded from ideation through deployment.

While the company’s foundation lies in deep, trust-based partnerships with German industrial leaders, its go-to-market strategy increasingly emphasizes international growth. In 2024, over 41% of revenues originated from outside Germany, with significant traction in the Nordics, France, North America, and Asia-Pacific. The company operates regional offices in Frankfurt, Hamburg, Paris, Amsterdam, Stockholm, and Singapore, each staffed with local sales, technical support, and regulatory compliance personnel. Looking ahead, Red Bill GmbH is poised for continued expansion, with a five-year roadmap centered on internationalization, product modularity, and AI-driven value creation. ## FOUNDING & HISTORY (1998-2025 detailed timeline)

**1998: Foundation and Early Vision**

Red Bill GmbH was formally incorporated in April 1998 by Dr. Johannes Keller and Sabine Fricke, two computer scientists and former Siemens engineers with a shared vision for digitizing Germany’s manufacturing backbone. Recognizing the nascent potential of the internet and networked software for industrial efficiency, they established the company’s headquarters at Theresienstraße 12, 80333 München—deliberately selecting this location for its proximity to the Technische Universität München (TUM) and the region’s established engineering firms. The company’s initial mission was to develop scalable software for automating plant maintenance and production scheduling, targeting Germany’s Mittelstand—a segment often underserved by global software giants.

**1999–2001: Bootstrapping and First Contracts**

Operating with a lean team of eight, Red Bill GmbH secured its first contract with Maschinenfabrik Huber AG, a Bavarian machinery supplier. The contract entailed the deployment of an early version of RedOps, a plant operations tool developed in Delphi and C++. This initial success established Red Bill’s reputation for reliability and technical depth. The company’s founders emphasized rigorous German engineering principles, investing heavily in quality assurance and documentation—practices that became ingrained in the company culture.

**2002–2005: Growth, Capitalization, and First SaaS Offering**

With Germany’s manufacturing sector embracing early digitalization, Red Bill GmbH grew its employee base to 28 and moved to a larger office within the same Theresienstraße building. In 2003, the company raised its first outside capital—a €2.5 million Series A round from Bayern Kapital and two Munich-based angel investors. This infusion enabled the development of BillCore, a modular ERP platform tailored for mid-sized industrial firms. In 2005, Red Bill launched BillCore as a hosted SaaS solution—one of the first in Germany to offer remote, subscription-based enterprise software—significantly lowering total cost of ownership for Mittelstand clients.

**2006–2009: Scaling, Industry Partnerships, and the Onset of Compliance Focus**

Red Bill GmbH’s strategic partnership with Siemens Digital Industries in 2006 provided access to Siemens’ channel network and reference customers, accelerating adoption across Bavaria and Baden-Württemberg. Revenues surpassed €10 million by 2008, and headcount reached 64. As the EU’s data protection regulations began to evolve, Red Bill proactively integrated privacy-by-design principles into its software architecture, laying the groundwork for future GDPR compliance. This period also saw the company’s first foray into international markets, with pilot deployments in Austria and Switzerland.

**2010–2012: Leadership in Industry 4.0 and Early AI Investments**

Anticipating the Industry 4.0 paradigm, Red Bill GmbH launched RedConnect, a secure IoT gateway enabling real-time data acquisition from industrial equipment. This positioned Red Bill as an early innovator in machine connectivity and predictive analytics. The company’s R&D center at Theresienstraße expanded to accommodate a growing team of data scientists and embedded systems engineers, raising total staff to 112 by the end of 2012. A strategic investment in AI research, initiated with a €1.2 million grant from the Bavarian Ministry of Economic Affairs, culminated in the prototyping of RedOps AI, an operations management suite with integrated machine learning capabilities.

**2013–2016: Expansion, Regulatory Readiness, and Cultural Transformation**

Red Bill GmbH’s customer base grew rapidly across Germany, with significant wins in the automotive, process manufacturing, and logistics sectors. The company reported €44 million in revenues by 2015. In anticipation of the EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Red Bill established an internal Data Governance Office and began rearchitecting its SaaS stack to ensure end-to-end data sovereignty and auditability. This compliance-first approach became a cornerstone of Red Bill’s differentiation. Internally, the company doubled down on Mittelstand values—long-term client relationships, apprenticeship programs, and a flat, collaborative hierarchy—while recruiting international talent to support product localization and global scalability.

**2017–2018: Internationalization and DORA/PSD2 Alignment**

With the introduction of the EU’s Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) and PSD2, Red Bill invested in regulatory technology (RegTech) modules, allowing clients to automate compliance reporting and risk monitoring. The company opened its first foreign sales office in Vienna, followed by Zurich and Milan. Employee count reached 273 by 2018. Red Bill’s Supervisory Board was reconstituted to include former DAX executives and compliance experts, strengthening governance in preparation for accelerated growth and capital market readiness.

**2019–2020: AI Automation Leadership and COVID-19 Adaptation**

Red Bill launched the next-generation RedOps AI platform in early 2019, integrating advanced neural networks for predictive maintenance and supply chain optimization. The COVID-19 pandemic posed significant operational challenges, but Red Bill’s cloud-first SaaS model and remote onboarding capabilities enabled uninterrupted service delivery. The company introduced a flexible work policy, investing in digital collaboration tools and employee wellness programs—critical factors in maintaining productivity and morale during lockdowns. Despite the global downturn, revenues grew 19% in 2020, reaching €132 million.

**2021: Growth Surge and Strategic Investments**

Propelled by the acceleration of digitalization across Europe, Red Bill GmbH secured a €45 million Series C funding round led by a syndicate of German institutional investors, including Deutsche Beteiligungs AG. The funds were earmarked for international expansion, R&D in AI explainability, and further strengthening of compliance features. The company launched Red Bill Academy, a corporate learning platform designed to upskill employees in AI, cybersecurity, and regulatory compliance. Employee headcount surpassed 600, with a third of staff based outside Germany.

**2022–2023: Global Expansion, ESG Initiatives, and DAX 40 Ambitions**

Red Bill GmbH opened regional hubs in London and Singapore, marking its evolution into a global SaaS provider. Revenue growth remained robust, reaching €347 million in 2023. The company formalized its DAX 40 ambitions, launching Project Ad Astra—a multi-year program focused on scaling operations, enhancing financial transparency, and embedding ESG (environmental, social, governance) metrics across the business. Red Bill published its first annual Sustainability Report, detailing initiatives in energy-efficient data centers, diversity hiring, and supply chain transparency. GDPR and DORA compliance were continuously audited, with zero regulatory breaches recorded.

**2024: Record Performance and Public Listing Preparations**

In 2024, Red Bill GmbH achieved a milestone €478 million in revenues and an EBITDA margin of 26.4%, driven by surging demand for enterprise SaaS and AI automation solutions. The company’s flagship products—RedOps AI, BillCore ERP, and RedConnect IoT Gateway—were now deployed in over 28 countries, serving both Mittelstand champions and multinational enterprises. Red Bill initiated preparations for a Frankfurt Stock Exchange IPO, engaging leading investment banks and legal advisors. Internal processes were aligned with DAX 40 listing requirements, including the implementation of IFRS financial reporting, enhanced Supervisory Board oversight, and the launch of a whistleblower protection system. Employee headcount reached 1,721 by year-end, representing 38 nationalities.

**2025: Scaling, Digital Sovereignty, and Future Outlook**

Entering 2025, Red Bill GmbH solidified its position as a European SaaS and AI automation leader. The company’s workforce grew to 1,847, reflecting continued investment in engineering, product management, and customer success teams. A new “Digital Sovereignty Lab” was established at the Munich headquarters, in collaboration with TUM, to research next-generation data privacy technologies and sovereign cloud architectures. Red Bill doubled down on its Mittelstand roots—launching a new apprenticeship program and localizing solutions for key European markets—while aggressively pursuing growth in North America and Asia-Pacific.

The company’s compliance posture remained exemplary, with GDPR and DORA audits confirming full alignment and zero non-conformities. The Supervisory Board expanded to include cybersecurity and international trade experts, further strengthening governance as Red Bill advanced towards its DAX 40 aspiration. Strategic partnerships with leading cloud providers, hardware OEMs, and regulatory bodies positioned Red Bill at the nexus of enterprise digital transformation and regulatory-driven innovation. 

Looking ahead, Red Bill GmbH’s founding ethos—combining German Mittelstand reliability with global ambition—continues to define its trajectory. As it prepares for public listing and further international expansion, the company remains committed to pioneering enterprise SaaS, AI automation, and Industry 4.0 solutions that empower customers to thrive in an increasingly digital, regulated, and interconnected world.

## ORGANIZATION STRUCTURE (full org chart)

Red Bill GmbH’s organization structure is meticulously crafted to balance the best practices of German Mittelstand governance, global enterprise scalability, and the agility required for rapid innovation in SaaS and AI. The company’s operational backbone is headquartered at Theresienstraße 12, 80333 München, with regional hubs and subsidiaries in key European and international markets. ### 1. Governance and Executive Leadership

At the apex of Red Bill GmbH’s structure is the dual-board system, in line with German corporate governance standards (AktG), comprising the Supervisory Board (Aufsichtsrat) and the Executive Board (Vorstand).

**Supervisory Board (Aufsichtsrat):**  
- Composed of 9 members, including the Chair (a former Siemens CTO), representatives from leading institutional investors, employee representatives (as per Mitbestimmungsgesetz), and independent experts in compliance and digital transformation.  
- Responsible for strategic oversight, appointment and supervision of the Executive Board, major capital allocation decisions, and compliance with DAX, EU, and German regulatory requirements (including GDPR and DORA).  
- Meets quarterly at the Munich HQ and annually for a strategy offsite.

**Executive Board (Vorstand):**  
- Led by the Chief Executive Officer (CEO), supported by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Chief Technology Officer (CTO), Chief Operating Officer (COO), and Chief Compliance & Risk Officer (CCRO).  
- Accountable for daily management, strategy execution, operational performance, and reporting to the Supervisory Board.  
- Executive Board members maintain dotted-line relationships with key functional leaders across global subsidiaries.

### 2. Executive Leadership Team (ELT)

Reporting to the Executive Board is the Executive Leadership Team (ELT), encompassing the following C-level and senior vice presidents (SVPs):

- **CEO (Dr. Annika Schuster):** Sets corporate vision, leads M&A and public markets strategy, oversees global expansion.

## FINANCIAL HISTORY (1998–2025: P&L, Balance Sheets, Cash Flow, Ratios)

### Early Formation and Foundation Years (1998–2004)

Red Bill GmbH was incorporated in 1998 at Theresienstraße 12, 80333 München, by a consortium of German software engineers and industry consultants. The late 1990s and early 2000s were characterized by modest seed capital of €1.2 million, largely sourced from founder equity and a grant from the Bavarian Ministry for Economic Affairs. The company’s initial focus was on bespoke process automation software for local manufacturing SMEs—a segment reflective of the traditional Bavarian Mittelstand.

**P&L:** Annual turnover in this period remained below €2 million, with marginal net profits as the company reinvested heavily in R&D and market development. COGS represented approximately 41–45% of revenues, primarily due to custom development and on-site implementation costs. Operating expenses were dominated by personnel costs (~58% of OPEX), consistent with Germany’s social security and wage structure.

**Balance Sheet:** Fixed assets comprised mostly IT infrastructure and leasehold improvements at Theresienstraße 12. Cash reserves fluctuated between €300,000 and €700,000, with no long-term debt. Equity financing provided a debt-to-equity ratio consistently below 0.1.

**Cash Flow:** Positive operating cash flow was achieved by 2003, with tight working capital management. Accounts receivable days averaged 38, reflecting the long payment cycles typical in German B2B.

**Key Ratios:** Gross margin: 55–59%. EBITDA margin: 8–12%. Current ratio: 2.1–2.4.

---

### Expansion, SaaS Pivot, and First Growth Phase (2005–2013)

The advent of cloud computing and the SaaS paradigm shift led Red Bill GmbH to reorient its product portfolio, investing heavily in multi-tenant architectures and scalable platforms. In 2007, a €6.5 million Series A led by a consortium of German and Swiss VCs enabled the company to double its engineering headcount and launch BillCore ERP.

**P&L:** Revenue grew from €4.2 million in 2005 to €32.7 million in 2013 (CAGR: ~29%), driven by SaaS subscriptions and maintenance contracts. Recurring revenue reached 61% by 2012. EBITDA margin improved steadily, from 10.3% (2005) to 18.1% (2013), as the business scaled and G&A costs were leveraged.

**Balance Sheet:** Capital expenditures increased to fund data center expansion and initial global office setups (Zurich, Vienna). Intangible assets (capitalized software development) rose to €7.8 million by 2013. No long-term bank debt; working capital financed via revolving credit lines with Commerzbank and Bayerische Landesbank.

**Cash Flow:** Operating cash flow turned robustly positive from 2009 onwards. Significant cash outflows were allocated to R&D (averaging 14% of sales), reflecting German “Forschungszulage” incentives.

**Key Ratios:** Gross margin: 62–66%. EBITDA margin: 10–18%. Net profit margin: 5–8%. Return on equity (ROE): 7–11%. Debt-to-equity: 0.13. Current ratio: 2.3.

---

### Maturity, Industry 4.0 Integration, and Globalization (2014–2020)

Red Bill GmbH’s entry into Industry 4.0 coincided with its first major international contracts and partnerships with DAX-listed manufacturing clients. The launch of RedOps AI and RedConnect IoT Gateway in 2016 catalyzed rapid growth, with a significant focus on EU data privacy compliance and cybersecurity (GDPR readiness project initiated in 2017).

**P&L:** Revenues surged from €39.5 million (2014) to €146.2 million (2020), with CAGR accelerating to 24%. Gross margin expanded to 69% as higher-margin AI modules and integration services gained traction. EBITDA margin reached 21.7% in 2020, supported by operational leverage and cloud migration. Net income increased from €2.7 million (2014) to €19.3 million (2020).

**Balance Sheet:** Asset base diversified to include significant software IP, cloud infrastructure leases, and an expanded international receivables portfolio.
